Muze

Turn everyday sounds into music with your face, hand, and touch.

1. Getting Started

Open MUZE on your phone (portrait mode). Tap "Touch to Begin" and allow camera access. The app loads 8 foley samples and the face/hand tracking models — this takes a few seconds on first load.

Once loaded, you'll see yourself on camera with a debug panel (top-left) and sample buttons (top-right). Music starts as soon as your face is detected.

2. Your Face = The Instrument

Your facial expressions shape the sound in real-time. You don't need to think about it — just emote naturally.

ExpressionWhat it does

The pad sound (sustained texture) plays automatically when your face is detected. It responds to your head movements and expressions. Just look at the camera and the music begins.

3. Your Hand = The Melody

Raise your free hand into the camera frame. A melody voice activates instantly.

GestureWhat it does
Hand up/downPitch goes up/down (quantized to the current scale — always in key)
Open palmLegato — notes sustain and blend smoothly
Closed fistStaccato — short, plucky, percussive notes
Hand leaves frameMelody fades out
The melody is always quantized to the scale your face is choosing. You literally cannot play a wrong note. Move your hand slowly for gentle melodies, quickly for expressive runs.

Your thumb on the screen controls rhythm and special effects.

The press-and-hold riser is the most satisfying gesture. Hold for 2-3 seconds to build tension, then release for the drop. Try it with a brow raise at the same moment for a chord change on the drop.

5. Samples — Your Sound Palette

MUZE turns everyday sounds into playable instruments. There are two sound slots:

Pad (sustained texture)

The pad loops a sample as a continuous, evolving sound. It plays when your face is detected. Your expressions shape it. Best with longer, tonal sounds.

Lead (pitched melody)

The lead pitch-shifts a sample across the musical scale. Your hand controls which note plays. Works with any sound — short percussive hits become plucky leads, longer tones become singing melodies.

Switching samples

Tap the "pad: ..." or "lead: ..." buttons in the top-right corner to cycle through all available samples. Any sample can be used for either slot — experiment!

Recording your own

Tap "record sample" — the app captures 2 seconds from your microphone. The recording is automatically assigned as your lead sound. Try:

  • Humming a note — becomes a vocal lead instrument
  • Clapping — becomes a percussive, punchy lead
  • Tapping a glass — becomes a crystalline bell sound
  • Saying "ahhh" — becomes a choir-like pad (switch to pad slot after recording)
  • Any ambient sound — your room, rain, traffic — becomes unique texture

6. Putting It All Together

Session flow

1. Open the app, tap to begin. Your face starts the pad texture.
2. Play with expressions — tilt your head up for brightness, smile for major key.
3. Raise your hand — a melody appears. Move slowly at first.
4. Double-tap for auto-drums. Swipe left/right to find a groove you like.
5. Now you're playing: face = mood, hand = melody, thumb = rhythm.
6. Try the hold-and-release for dramatic builds. Swipe up for reverb washes.
7. Quick brow raise to change chords when it feels right.
8. Record something around you and play it as your lead.

Combos to try

ComboEffect

Every session is unique because it's driven by YOUR expressions. Nobody else will create the same music you do. The system is designed so that everything always sounds good — you can't make a wrong move.

7. Tips

Lighting: Face tracking works best in even, front-facing light. Avoid strong backlighting.

Distance: Keep your face roughly centered in the frame, about arm's length from the phone.

Hand tracking: Works best with a clear background behind your hand. Spread fingers clearly for open palm detection.

Headphones: Recommended for the best experience — you'll hear more detail in the effects and avoid feedback when recording samples.

Portrait mode: The app is designed for portrait orientation on mobile.
