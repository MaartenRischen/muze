I First Touch

Make music in 30 seconds

This section gets you playing immediately. No setup, no menus. Just music.

1. Press Play

Open Jammerman on your phone in portrait mode. Tap "Begin Session" and allow camera access. The app loads face and hand tracking AI models (a few seconds on first load). Once ready, tap the play button (top right). Music starts immediately — a warm pad, a gentle arpeggio, and drums all respond to your face from the first moment.

Use headphones for the best experience. You'll hear spatial effects, binaural beats, and stereo details that speakers miss.

2. Smile & Frown

Smile — the music gets brighter and more uplifting. The scale shifts toward lydian and ionian modes. Frown — it gets darker and more tense, moving toward phrygian and aeolian. A neutral face lands on dorian, which is bittersweet and versatile. Watch the mode name change in the top-left corner, and notice the colors shift across the whole interface. Your emotional expression is literally choosing the musical mode in real-time.

3. Move Your Hand

Hold up your free hand to the camera. A melody synth activates instantly and follows your hand position — higher hand = higher pitch, lower hand = lower pitch. Every note is quantized to the current scale, so you literally cannot play a wrong note.

GestureEffect
Open palmSmooth legato — notes sustain and blend
Closed fistShort staccato — plucky, percussive notes
Hand leaves frameMelody fades out gracefully

4. Tap Drums

The lower half of the screen is divided into three invisible drum zones:

Screen AreaSound
Top thirdHi-hat / cymbal (open hat every 4th hit)
Middle thirdSnare / clap (layered noise + pitched body)
Bottom thirdKick drum (membrane + transient, triggers sidechain pump)

Tap to play live drum hits. Tap velocity is dynamic — faster taps hit harder. Double-tap anywhere in the drum zone to start an auto-rhythm. Double-tap again to stop. An "AUTO" indicator appears when active.

5. Change Chords

The bottom bar has 6 chord buttons: I ii iii IV V vi. Tap any chord and everything follows — the pad shifts, the arpeggio rebuilds, the melody re-quantizes, all in key. You are now performing a live, expressive, face-controlled musical instrument.

There is no wrong move. Every note is in key. Every gesture makes music. Just play.

II Exploring

All face, hand & touch controls

Now that you're making music, here is every expression and gesture control at your disposal.

1. Head Tilt = Filter

Tilt your chin down — the sound gets muffled and warm as a resonant lowpass filter closes. Chin up or forward — bright and open, more presence and attack. This filter is volume-compensated so it stays balanced at any angle. It affects the pad, arpeggio, and melody simultaneously. This single gesture gives you incredible dynamic range — it is probably the most important expression in Jammerman.

2. Eyes = Space

Open your eyes wide — reverb and delay increase dramatically, creating a spacious, expansive wash. Squint — the sound becomes dry and intimate, close and personal. This controls the perceived "room size" of the entire tonal mix. The reverb has subtle chorus modulation for lush tails. The ring visualization also responds to eye openness.

3. Head Roll = Shimmer

Tilt your head sideways (left or right ear toward shoulder). Chorus depth increases, making the pad shimmer and widen in stereo. The more extreme the tilt, the deeper the effect. Great for dreamy, atmospheric moments.

4. Eyebrows = Octave

Raise your eyebrows to shift the melody and arpeggio up an octave. Lower them to come back down. Combine this with hand position for a huge overall pitch range. Raising eyebrows while your hand is high puts the melody into sparkling territory.

5. Riser & Drop

The signature performance gesture. Press and hold your finger on any drum zone for 400ms or more. A filtered noise sweep begins building — it gets louder and brighter over 4 seconds. Drums and pad duck automatically to create tension.

ActionResult
Hold 400ms+Riser builds, drums & pad duck
Swipe up (while holding)THE DROP — massive kick hit + full reverb wash on all channels
Release (without swipe)Cancel — everything fades back smoothly
Hold for 2-3 seconds to build tension, then swipe up and simultaneously change chord for maximum impact. This is the most satisfying gesture in the app.

6. Tape Stop

Swipe down on a drum zone for a tape stop effect. The BPM drops, pitch falls two octaves, everything slows to a crawl — then snaps back to normal. Dramatic for transitions and for ending phrases.

7. Reverb Throw

Swipe up quickly (without holding first) for a reverb throw. The current sound explodes into a massive reverb wash that fades naturally. Unlike the riser+drop, this is instant — great for punctuating moments or adding space between phrases.

8. Drum Patterns

With auto-rhythm running (double-tap to activate), swipe left or right in the drum zone to cycle through 6+ drum patterns:

PatternCharacter
BasicStandard four-on-the-floor
FunkSyncopated, groovy
BrokenIrregular, glitchy
MinimalSparse, spacious
TrapHi-hat rolls, heavy kick
HalftimeSnare on beat 3, slow feel at double BPM

9. Beat Repeat

Triple-tap any drum zone to trigger a 2-second stutter effect on that drum sound. The repeat accelerates automatically: starts at 8th notes, speeds to 16th notes, then 32nd notes. Velocity increases as speed increases. A pulsing visual flash overlay appears during the effect. Perfect for build-ups right before a drop or chord change.

10. Visual Effects

Jammerman is an audio-visual instrument. Everything you see reacts to the music in real-time:

  • Face contour — A glowing outline traces your face landmarks, colored by the current mode
  • Iris lights — Your eyes glow based on reverb/delay amount
  • Hand trail — A light trail follows your hand, showing pitch position
  • Ring visualization — The circular visualization pulses with the beat and reacts to eye openness
  • Mood lighting — Edge glows and gradient overlays shift with the musical mode
  • Beat flash — Subtle screen flash on kick and snare hits
  • Background blur — AI-powered bokeh keeps your face sharp while the background softens
The visual effects are rendered on a canvas overlay. When you record video, all of these visuals are captured alongside the audio.

Shape your instrument. Everything here is accessible while you play.

Tap the gear icon (top right) to open the synth panel. It has five tabs:

3. Tempo & Swing

In the Perform tab: drag the BPM slider (40-200) to set tempo. Use the TAP TEMPO button for live tempo matching — tap it rhythmically and Jammerman will calculate the BPM. The swing slider (0-80%) adds shuffle feel, offsetting every other beat. Swing is especially good with lo-fi and halftime presets.

5. The Mixer

Tap MIX (top right) to open a professional mixing desk. 8 channel strips plus a master bus:

Channels: Pad, Arp, Melody, Kick, Snare, Hat, Binaural, Riser, Master

Each channel has:

  • Custom fader (drag to adjust volume, -60dB to +6dB)
  • Real-time level meter with peak hold
  • Pan indicator
  • Mute (M) and Solo (S) buttons

6. Channel Detail

In the mixer, tap any channel label to open its detail panel with large rotary-style controls:

  • 3-band EQ — Low, Mid, High gain controls
  • Reverb send — How much signal goes to the shared reverb return
  • Delay send — How much signal goes to the BPM-synced delay
  • Pan — Stereo position

The master bus has: 3-band EQ, master gain, limiter threshold, and tape saturation (always-on subtle warmth). Tonal channels (pad, arp, melody) are "face-linked" — their reverb/delay sends are modulated by your eye openness in real-time. Drum channels have fixed sends.

Solo a channel (S button) to hear it in isolation. This is great for dialing in EQ or checking what each layer contributes to the mix.

7. Pad & Lead Samples

8. Binaural Beats

In the Binaural tab: toggle on for a subtle low-frequency binaural beat. Choose between two modes:

ModeBehavior
TonicFixed root pitch — stable, meditative drone
ChordFollows current harmony — binaural frequency shifts with each chord change

Adjust the beat frequency (1-20 Hz) for different mental states. Lower frequencies (1-4 Hz, delta/theta) for deep relaxation. Higher frequencies (12-20 Hz, beta) for alertness and focus.

IV Performance

Loops, scenes & live techniques

These tools turn Jammerman from an instrument into a live performance system.

1. Loop Your Melody

In the Perform tab, tap REC to start the melody loop recorder. A 1-bar count-in plays (you'll hear click cues), then your hand melody is recorded for the set number of bars. When the recording completes, it loops back automatically. Tap OVR (overdub) to add more layers on top. Each layer stacks. UNDO removes the last layer. X clears everything.

The progress bar at the top shows the loop position with a smooth animated gradient. Colors indicate state: green = playing, red = recording, amber = overdubbing.

Build a full arrangement live: record a hand melody, overdub a second melody line, switch presets, overdub again. Each layer stacks. This is how you go from nothing to a complete song in 30 seconds.

2. Loop Length

Change the loop length before recording by cycling through 1, 2, 4, or 8 bars. Shorter loops create tight, repetitive phrases. Longer loops give more freedom for melodic development. Match the loop length to your musical idea — 1 bar for a riff, 4 bars for a full melody, 8 bars for a complete progression.

3. Save Scenes

In the Perform tab, tap SAVE then a slot (1-4) to snapshot your entire instrument setup:

  • BPM, key, scale, swing
  • Chord position
  • All channel volumes, pans, EQ settings
  • Reverb/delay send levels
  • Synth oscillator types and ADSR envelopes
  • Master volume and preset state

Tap a saved slot to crossfade to that scene over 2 seconds. All parameters morph smoothly. Active slots glow with the current accent color.

Set up 4 different vibes (intro, verse, chorus, drop) and switch between them live. The 2-second crossfade keeps everything musical — no abrupt changes.

4. Chord Auto-Advance

In the Perform tab, toggle CHORDS to auto-cycle through the progression I → ii → iii → IV → V → vi every bar. A small arrow appears on the chord bar showing what's coming next. This is powerful combined with the loop recorder — record a loop with auto-advance running to capture a full harmonic progression that keeps cycling.

5. Gyroscope

Tap the gyro button to enable phone tilt control (mobile only, requires permission):

TiltEffect
Left/rightPans arp and melody in opposite stereo directions for width
Forward/backModulates reverb depth on all tonal channels

A subtle indicator appears showing tilt and pitch values. All values are smoothed for musical results.

6. Record Video

Tap the record button (circle, top right) to capture your performance as video with audio. The recording captures the camera feed, all visual effects (face contour, hand trail, ring visualization, mood lighting), and the full audio mix at 720p.

ModeOutput
STEREO720p video with stereo audio mix — ready to share
MULTIVideo + separate audio files for pad, arp, melody, drums — for DAW mixing

On iOS, you can share the recording directly to your camera roll or any app. A REC indicator and data overlay appear in the recorded video.

7. Performance Flow

Here is a template for a great live set:

  1. Start with a preset (Ambient Dream for chill, Dark Techno for energy)
  2. Set your key and BPM. Enable chord auto-advance if you want harmonic movement.
  3. Play with face expressions — find a mood, a mode that resonates.
  4. Enable auto-drums, try different patterns (swipe left/right).
  5. Build a hand melody loop. Tap REC, play for 4 bars.
  6. Save the whole setup to Scene 1.
  7. Switch preset, change key, maybe change scale. Build a second loop.
  8. Save to Scene 2.
  9. Now perform: crossfade between scenes, use riser/drop, beat repeat, reverb throw.
  10. Hit record to capture the whole thing as video.

8. Master It

Every parameter stacks and interacts. Face controls the mood. Hand plays the melody. Touch drives rhythm and effects. Tilt adds spatial expression. Scenes give you structure. The loop recorder gives you memory. There are no wrong moves — only your unique performance. The system is designed so that everything always sounds good. Go perform.

* Tips & Troubleshooting

Lighting: Face tracking works best in even, front-facing light. Avoid strong backlighting or silhouette situations.

Distance: Keep your face roughly centered, about arm's length from the phone. The AI models are optimized for this range.

Hand tracking: Works best with a clear background behind your hand. Spread fingers clearly for open palm detection. One hand only — the other holds the phone.

Headphones: Strongly recommended. You'll hear stereo panning, binaural beats, spatial effects, and avoid feedback when recording.

Portrait mode: The app is designed for portrait orientation on mobile. Landscape works but the drum zones shift.

Background blur: The app automatically blurs your background using AI segmentation. Your face stays sharp, the background becomes beautiful bokeh.

Auto-save: All settings save to your browser automatically. Close and reopen — your BPM, key, scale, presets, mixer, and scenes are restored.

Freeze mode: Tap the snowflake button next to the mode HUD to lock the current scale. Your face still controls filter, reverb, and chorus, but the scale won't change. Useful when you find a perfect mode.

Desktop: Jammerman works on desktop with a webcam. Mouse clicks work on chord buttons and the toolbar. Touch gestures (drum zones, swipes) require a touchscreen.

& Audio Architecture

Signal chain: Synths → Channel EQ → Panner → Gain → Reverb/Delay sends (post-fader) | Tonal channels route through face-linked master filter | All channels → Tape Saturation (2x oversample) → Master EQ → Master Gain → Limiter → Analyser → Output. Reverb return has subtle chorus modulation for lush tails. Delay syncs to BPM via dotted-8th notation. Kick triggers sidechain compression on the pad channel for rhythmic pumping.
