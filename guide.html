1. Getting Started

Basics

Open MUZE on your phone in portrait mode. Tap "Begin Session" and allow camera access. The app loads face and hand tracking models (a few seconds on first load).

The app starts paused. Tap the play button (top-right) to begin. Your face starts the pad texture immediately. The music responds to everything you do.

There is no wrong move. Every note is in key. Every gesture makes music. Just play.

Use headphones for the best experience. You'll hear spatial effects, binaural beats, and stereo details that speakers miss.

2. Your Face = The Instrument

Basics

Your facial expressions shape the sound in real-time. You don't need to think about it — just emote naturally.

ExpressionWhat it does
Tilt head upOpens the filter — sound gets brighter, more present
Tilt head downCloses the filter — warm, muffled, intimate
SmileShifts to brighter scales (lydian, ionian, mixolydian)
FrownShifts to darker scales (aeolian, phrygian)
Neutral faceDorian mode — versatile, bittersweet
Raise eyebrowsMelody and arp play in higher octave

SquintLess reverb — dry, intimate, close

Open mouthOpens the arp filter — brighter, more biting arpeggios
The pad plays automatically when your face is detected. Your head pitch controls a resonant lowpass filter with volume compensation. Head tilt up = bright, chin down = warm. This single gesture gives you incredible dynamic range.

3. Your Hand = The Melody

Basics

Raise your free hand into the camera frame. A melody synth activates instantly.

GestureWhat it does
Hand up/downPitch goes up/down (always in key — quantized to current scale)
Open palmLegato — notes sustain and blend smoothly
Closed fistStaccato — short, plucky, percussive notes
Hand leaves frameMelody fades out gracefully
You literally cannot play a wrong note. The melody is always quantized to whatever scale your face is choosing. Move slowly for gentle melodies, quickly for expressive runs.

4. Touch = Drums

Basics

The screen is divided into three invisible drum zones:

Screen AreaSound
Top thirdHi-hat / cymbal (with open hat variation every 4th hit)
Middle thirdSnare / clap (layered: noise top + pitched body)
Bottom thirdKick drum (layered: membrane + click transient, triggers sidechain pump)

Tap velocity is dynamic — faster taps = harder hits.

GestureWhat it does
Single tapPlays a drum hit in that zone
Double-tapToggles auto-rhythm on/off (shows "AUTO" indicator)
Swipe left/rightCycle through 6 drum patterns (Basic, Funk, Broken, Minimal, Trap, Halftime)
Every kick hit subtly ducks the pad volume (sidechain pumping) for that classic groove feel. It happens automatically.

5. Touch Effects

Intermediate

Beyond basic drums, the touch zone supports powerful performance gestures:

GestureWhat it does
Press & hold (400ms+)Riser — filtered noise sweeps up, drums duck over 4 seconds
Release hold + swipe upThe Drop — big kick, reverb wash on all channels, drums restore
Release hold (no swipe)Cancel riser — everything fades back smoothly
Swipe up (quick)Reverb throw — sends all channels into huge reverb, fades back
Swipe downTape stop — BPM drops, pitch drops 2 octaves, snaps back
Triple-tapBeat repeat — 2-second stutter (8th → 16th → 32nd notes)
The press-and-hold riser is the most satisfying gesture. Hold for 2-3 seconds to build tension, then swipe up for the drop. Try combining it with a chord change for maximum impact.

6. Sound Palette

Intermediate

MUZE has two sound slots that can use synth engines or samples:

Pad (sustained texture)

The pad plays when your face is detected. Default is an FM synth with sub oscillator and detuned unison layer for width. You can switch it to any sample — it becomes a granular looping texture.

Lead (pitched melody)

The lead follows your hand position. Default is a mono synth with filter envelope and optional vibrato.

9. The Mixer

Advanced

Tap MIX (top-right) to open a professional 8-channel mixer + master bus:

Channels: Pad, Arp, Melody, Kick, Snare, Hat, Binaural, Riser, Master

Each channel has:

  • Custom fader (drag to adjust volume, -60dB to +6dB)
  • Real-time level meter with peak hold
  • Pan indicator
  • Mute (M) and Solo (S) buttons
  • Tap the channel label for the detail view: 3-band EQ, reverb send, delay send, and pan

The master bus has: 3-band EQ, gain, limiter threshold, tape saturation (always-on, subtle warmth), and a modulated reverb return for lush tails.

Tonal channels (pad, arp, melody) are "face-linked" — their reverb/delay sends are modulated by your eye openness in real-time. Drum channels are fixed sends. Solo a channel to hear it isolated.

11. Loop Recorder

Advanced

The loop bar (left side) turns MUZE into a live looping instrument. It captures 4 bars of audio at the current BPM.

ActionWhat it does
Tap circleStart recording. Auto-stops after 4 bars.
Tap again (playing)Start overdub — new layer on top of existing loop.
+ buttonAlso starts overdub while playing.
Undo arrowRemove the last overdub layer.
X buttonClear all layers and stop.

The progress bar shows loop position with a smooth animated gradient and playhead. Colors change: green = playing, red = recording, amber = overdubbing.

Build a full arrangement live: record drums first, overdub pad, add melody, switch presets or keys, overdub again. Each layer stacks. This is how you go from nothing to a complete song in 30 seconds.

12. Scenes (4 Slots)

Advanced

Save your entire instrument state and recall it instantly. Scene slots are at the bottom-left.

ActionWhat it does
Tap SAVEEnter save mode (slots pulse with accent glow). Tap a slot to save.
Tap saved slotRecall that scene with a smooth 2-second crossfade.

A scene saves: BPM, key, scale, swing, chord position, all channel volumes, pans, EQ, reverb/delay sends, synth oscillator types, master volume, and preset state.

Set up 4 different vibes (verse, chorus, bridge, drop) and switch between them live. The 2-second crossfade keeps everything musical. Active slots glow with the current accent color.

13. Chord Auto-Advance

Performance

Tap the CHORDS button in the performance bar to enable automatic chord progression.

When active, chords cycle through the progression every bar: I → ii → iii → IV → V → vi → repeat.

A small arrow indicator appears above the next chord in the progression so you always know what's coming.

Combine chord auto-advance with a loop recording to capture a full 6-chord progression. Then disable it and play freely over your recorded harmony.

14. Beat Repeat / Stutter

Performance

Triple-tap any drum zone to trigger a 2-second beat repeat effect on that drum sound.

The stutter accelerates automatically: starts at 8th notes, speeds to 16th notes, then 32nd notes. Velocity increases as speed increases. A visual flash overlay pulses during the effect.

Use beat repeat right before a drop for an incredible build-up. Triple-tap the kick zone, let it build for 2 seconds, then hit a chord change.

17. Session Flow

Basics

Quick start (30 seconds)

1. Open app, tap Begin Session, tap play.
2. Your face starts the pad — tilt head, smile, frown. Music responds.
3. Raise your hand — melody appears. Move slowly.
4. Double-tap for auto-drums. You're making music.

Full performance (5 minutes)

1. Start with a preset (Ambient Dream for chill, Dark Techno for energy).
2. Set your key and BPM. Enable chord auto-advance.
3. Play with face expressions — find a mood.
4. Enable auto-drums, try different patterns (swipe left/right).
5. Add hand melody — explore the range.
6. Record a loop: tap the loop circle, play for 4 bars, it auto-stops.
7. Overdub: while loop plays, tap + button, add melody layer.
8. Save to Scene 1.
9. Switch preset, change key. Save to Scene 2.
10. Now perform: switch between scenes, use riser/drop, beat repeat, reverb throw.
11. Hit record to capture the whole thing.

19. Tips

Lighting: Face tracking works best in even, front-facing light. Avoid strong backlighting.

Distance: Keep your face roughly centered, about arm's length from the phone.

Hand tracking: Works best with a clear background behind your hand. Spread fingers clearly for open palm detection.

Headphones: Strongly recommended — you'll hear stereo panning, binaural beats, spatial effects, and avoid feedback when recording.

Portrait mode: The app is designed for portrait orientation on mobile.

Background blur: The app automatically blurs your background using AI segmentation. Your face stays sharp, the background becomes a beautiful bokeh.

Saving: All settings auto-save to your browser. Close and reopen — your BPM, key, scale, presets, and scenes are restored.

Freeze mode: Tap the snowflake button next to the mode HUD to lock the current scale. Your face still controls filter, reverb, and chorus, but the scale won't change. Useful when you find a perfect mode.

Audio Architecture

Signal chain: Synths → Channel EQ → Panner → Gain → Reverb/Delay sends (post-fader) | Tonal channels route through face-linked master filter | All channels → Tape Saturation (2x oversample) → Master EQ → Master Gain → Limiter → Analyser → Output. Reverb return has subtle chorus modulation for lush tails. Delay syncs to BPM via dotted-8th notation.
